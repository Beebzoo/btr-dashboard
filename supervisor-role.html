Research Supervisor Role & Responsibilities

Your comprehensive guide to supervising BTR students through their thesis research journey

Overview: The Bachelor Thesis Research

The Bachelor Thesis Research (BTR) is a one-semester, 30 ECTS research project that marks the completion of the Maastricht Science Programme degree. Students specialize in a scientific area of their choosing and experience the entire scientific cycle: formulating research questions, designing experiments, analyzing results, and communicating findings.

Students may conduct their BTR with MSP staff, at other Maastricht University locations, or at another institution within or outside the Netherlands.

Your Role as Research Supervisor

As the Research Supervisor, you provide support, feedback, and assessment throughout the BTR. You are the primary point of contact for the student's research journey.

Key Responsibilities

1. Support & Guidance

  • Introduce the student to the specifics of your research area
  • Discuss and guide experimental design
  • Provide instruction on equipment, approaches, and methodologies
  • Teach good working practices and result analysis
  • Foster student independence while ensuring scientific quality

2. Regular Contact & Monitoring

  • Essential: Maintain regular contact to monitor student progress
  • Provide semi-structured feedback at key assessment points
  • Hold face-to-face meetings for proposal and progress discussions
  • Be available for questions and guidance throughout the semester

3. Feedback on Written Work

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Feedback Limitation

To ensure a "level playing field" for all students:

  • Proposal: Provide feedback on ONE draft only (not more)
  • Final Thesis: Provide feedback on ONE draft only (not more)
  • Feedback may be given on parts as the student writes, or on the entire document at once
  • This ensures the work reflects the student's own capabilities

4. Supervisor Qualifications

  • Research supervisors should have several years of research experience
  • PhD required: Supervisors are generally expected to have a PhD
  • PhD students may act as day-to-day supervisors but cannot be official supervisors with final BTR responsibility

Assessment & Grading Responsibilities

As Research Supervisor, you are responsible for grading four of the five BTR components:

Components You Grade

1. Research Proposal (15% of total grade)

  • Graded with: Internal Advisor
  • Deadline: ~3 weeks after proposal submission
  • What to assess: Literature review, aims/hypotheses, methodology, anticipated outcomes, research importance, ethical considerations
  • Word limit: Max 2000 words (strictly enforced - BTR office applies penalties)
  • Required citations: Minimum 15 peer-reviewed journal articles

2. Midpoint Practical Grade (15% of total grade)

  • Graded by: You alone
  • When: Approximately halfway through BTR
  • What to assess: Practical skills, independence, problem-solving abilities, research competence
  • Important: Hold a meeting with the student to discuss this grade and allow them to improve

3. Written Thesis (25% of total grade)

  • Graded with: Internal Advisor
  • Deadline: ~5 weeks after thesis submission
  • What to assess: Scientific quality, writing, analysis, conclusions, literature integration
  • Word limit: Max 15,000 words (excluding references and appendices)
  • Extensions: Only granted with written approval from you, communicated to Internal Advisor and BTR Office 48 hours before submission

4. Final Practical Grade (35% of total grade)

  • Graded by: You alone
  • Includes: Overall research execution + Research Group Presentation
  • Presentation timing: Approximately one week before or after thesis submission (flexible if needed, but must be completed before final grading deadline)
  • Your discretion: Determine presentation expectations (length, content, format)

Component You Do NOT Grade

5. BTR Video (10% of total grade)

  • Graded by: Internal Advisor and a peer student
  • Length: 5-7 minutes (content after 7 minutes is not graded)
  • Purpose: Communicate research to non-scientists
  • Your involvement: Optional - your input is welcome but not required

üéì Understanding the Dutch Grading System

Grades range from 1.0 (lowest) to 10.0 (highest), reported to one decimal place:

5.5 = Minimum passing grade
7.5 = Average / satisfactory
8.0+ = Very good
9.0+ = Excellent
9.5+ = Exceptional
10.0 = Perfect (very rare!)

Note: Students must achieve a weighted average of 5.5 or higher across all components to pass the BTR and earn 30 ECTS credits. Individual components may be below 5.5 if compensated by other grades.

Grading Procedures & Platform

Using Collent for Grading

All BTR submissions and grading are managed through Collent, the university's submission platform.

How It Works:

  • You'll receive automatic email notifications when your student submits work
  • Access grading rubrics directly in Collent
  • Submit grades through Collent by the specified deadlines
  • Plagiarism and word count checks are performed by the BTR Office

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: When Multiple Supervisors Are Involved

If a student is supervised by multiple individuals or guided by a PhD student assistant:

  • The main Research Supervisor must process final grades in Collent
  • Consult with co-supervisors/assistants but submit grades under your name
  • You retain final responsibility for all assessments

Handling Grading Discrepancies

When you and the Internal Advisor grade jointly (Proposal and Thesis):

  • If your grades differ by more than 1.5 points, the BTR Office will ask you to discuss and reach consensus
  • Grades don't necessarily need to change, but discussion ensures understanding of different perspectives
  • Do not communicate grades to students until the BTR Office confirms there are no discrepancy issues
  • If consensus cannot be reached quickly, the BTR Coordinator reserves the right to make a final decision

Providing Feedback to Students

After grades are released (students can see them in Collent):

  • You must provide feedback to help the student improve
  • Face-to-face meetings are strongly urged
  • Discuss strengths, weaknesses, and areas for improvement
  • For proposal feedback: help guide improvements for the final thesis

Deadline Importance

‚è∞ Critical: Submit Grades On Time

Grade deadlines are strict due to graduation requirements:

  • Late grades can delay student graduation
  • Financial implications for students (tuition, housing, job starts)
  • Master's program admissions may be affected
  • Contact BTR Office immediately if you foresee any delays

Additional Important Information

The Internal Advisor's Role

Each student is assigned an Internal Advisor (usually MSP or allied staff) who:

  • Verifies scientific quality of proposed experiments and outcomes
  • Is NOT necessarily an expert in the research area
  • Does NOT typically contribute to thesis development (except in general terms)
  • Grades the Proposal and Final Thesis alongside you
  • Meets with student to discuss progress, work ethic, and integration
  • May contact you to discuss student progress if needed

Additional Modules During BTR

Students may request to take one additional module (max 5 ECTS) during their BTR:

  • Your approval required along with Board of Examiners approval
  • You are NOT obligated to support such requests
  • Student must discuss workload management with you before BTR begins
  • Only allowed for BTRs in/near Maastricht (not abroad)
  • If you don't approve, or if requested after BTR starts, module must be completed after BTR ends

External BTRs: Legal Agreements

For students conducting BTRs outside Maastricht University:

  • Internship agreement required between UM, host institution, and student
  • Student CANNOT start until agreement is fully signed
  • You should receive this document from the student
  • Please review and complete as early as possible
  • Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) also require legal review
  • Documents submitted less than 1 month before BTR start may cause delays
  • Contact BTR Office with any questions or concerns

Student Compensation

Students generally cannot receive financial compensation for their BTR work, except:

  • Reimbursement of expenses
  • Certain special grants (e.g., Erasmus grants)
  • Any other compensation must be discussed with BTR Coordinator well in advance

Supervisor Compensation

For external (non-UM) research groups:

  • No financial compensation provided
  • Research costs are not covered by UM
  • Students provide valuable research contributions at no cost

For internal (UM) supervisors:

  • Minimal teaching hours credited for educational efforts
  • Hours do not fully cover supervision time required

When Problems Arise

If issues occur during the BTR (personal problems, research issues, attendance problems):

  • Student or Research Supervisor should contact the Internal Advisor or BTR Coordinator
  • Severity determines who handles the situation
  • Extensions/special accommodations: BTR Coordinator decides after consulting you
  • Student's responsibility to request extensions (not yours), but your input is essential

If a Student Fails

If a student receives a final grade below 5.5:

  • MSP may offer a resit or require them to start again
  • MSP handles the process
  • You are NOT obligated to take the student back if they fail
