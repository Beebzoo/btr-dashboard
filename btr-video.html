üéØ What Makes Your Video Different

‚ñº
This is NOT a Research Presentation

Your BTR video is fundamentally different from academic presentations or your thesis defense. It's aimed at communicating your research to non-scientists, not experts in your field.

‚ùå Don't Do This

  • Use complex, difficult-to-explain scientific terms
  • Assume audience has specialized knowledge
  • Focus primarily on technical methodology
  • Record your defense presentation and submit that

‚úì Do This Instead

  • Explain concepts in simple, accessible language
  • Use analogies and examples anyone can understand
  • Focus on why your research matters to society
  • Make it engaging and relatable

Your Target Audience

Think of your video as being watched by:

üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß Family Members

Your parents or grandparents (unless they're scientists!)

üéì High School Students

Someone interested in science but without specialist training

üèõÔ∏è Policy Makers

People deciding on research funding priorities

üì∞ Journalists

Writers covering scientific advances for the public

Your Goal:

Make them understand and care about your research, even without scientific background.

üìù Required Content Elements

‚ñº

Your video must address four key elements. Each is essential and will be assessed.

1. Scientific Importance ‚öóÔ∏è

Key Question:

How does this work increase our knowledge of the world/universe?

What to Explain:

  • What gap in scientific understanding does it fill?
  • What didn't we know before that we know now (or will know)?
  • How does it fit into the bigger scientific picture?

Example Framing:

"Scientists have known X for years, but we didn't understand Y. My research helps us understand Y by..."

2. Societal Importance üåç

Key Question:

What contribution does your work make to a better world?

Applied Research

  • Health improvements
  • Environmental benefits
  • Technological advances
  • Economic benefits

"Blue Sky" Research

  • Why this knowledge matters
  • Long-term benefits
  • Unexpected applications
  • Human understanding

3. Ethical Considerations ‚öñÔ∏è

CRITICAL:

There is no piece of science free from ethical considerations. You MUST address this.

Common Ethical Areas:

  • Living Subjects: Animal welfare, human rights, consent, potential harm
  • Data Ethics: Privacy, anonymity, security, access, long-term management
  • Resource Allocation: Best use of funding, opportunity costs
  • Environmental Impact: Energy consumption, waste, materials, long-term effects
  • Dual-Use Concerns: Potential for misuse, responsibility in dissemination
  • Social Justice: Who benefits, who might be harmed, equity considerations

‚ùå Never Say

"My research has no ethical considerations"

‚úì Always Say

"The key ethical considerations in my work are..."

4. Clear Structure & Storytelling üìñ

Opening Statement

  • Hook the audience immediately
  • State clearly what your research is about
  • Why should they care?

Middle (Main Content)

  • Logically organized sections
  • Clear transitions between ideas
  • Progressive building of understanding
  • Visual aids to support explanations

Conclusion

  • Summarize key takeaways
  • Reinforce why it matters
  • Leave audience with clear understanding

Video Format Requirements

Specification Requirement
File Format MP4 (MPEG-4)
Duration 5-7 minutes (videos significantly shorter or longer may be penalized)
File Size Maximum 2GB

Quality Requirements

üé• Video Quality

  • Clean background
  • No shaking (use tripod)
  • Good lighting

üîä Audio Quality

  • No background noise

  • Consistent volume
  • No echo

Poor audio quality will significantly impact your grade and audience engagement. Record in a quiet environment with good equipment.

üé≠ Presentation & Delivery

‚ñº

Your On-Camera Presence Matters

‚úì Professional Appearance

  • Dress appropriately but comfortably
  • Use solid colors (avoid checkered patterns)
  • Good posture - sit/stand straight
  • Face clearly visible
  • Look well-groomed and prepared

‚úì Engaging Delivery

  • Maintain high energy levels
  • Show enthusiasm for your work
  • Look into the camera (eye contact)
  • Appropriate facial expressions
  • Avoid appearing bored or robotic

Communication Style

The Golden Rules:
  • No jargon - explain everything in accessible terms
  • Use examples and analogies to illustrate complex points
  • Stick to the point - don't ramble or digress
  • Match tone to subject - humor is fine for some topics, not others
  • Pace yourself - not too fast, not too slow

Visual Elements

‚ö†Ô∏è Important:

We want a video, not a PowerPoint presentation. Your face must appear at some point in the video.

Using PowerPoint:

  • ‚úì Acceptable: Using PowerPoint slides as part of your video
  • ‚úó Not Acceptable: Entire video being only PowerPoint slides
  • ‚úó Not Acceptable: Submitting PowerPoint file directly
Best Practice:

Mix of direct presentation (you speaking to camera) and visual aids (slides, images, diagrams). Consider picture-in-picture with you in a corner while showing visuals.

‚ùì Common Questions & Troubleshooting

‚ñº

Technical Issues

Q: My video file is over 2GB. What do I do?

Solution:
  • Export at lower bitrate (5-8 Mbps often sufficient)
  • Reduce resolution if necessary (720p instead of 1080p)
  • Use H.264 codec for efficient compression
  • Check for "web" or "online" export presets in editing software

Q: How do I convert PowerPoint to MP4?

In PowerPoint:
  1. File ‚Üí Export ‚Üí Create a Video
  2. Choose quality (1080p or 720p)
  3. Set timings or record narration
  4. Save as MPEG-4 video

Q: WeTransfer link expired before grading. What now?

This is why SURFdrive backup is mandatory:

Assessors can access your video from SURFdrive. Make sure your SURFdrive upload succeeded.

Content Questions

Q: What if my research doesn't have obvious societal importance?

All research has value to society:
  • Explain how fundamental knowledge advances human understanding
  • Discuss potential long-term applications (even if speculative)
  • Give examples of "useless" research that became transformative
  • Explain why society benefits from funding curiosity-driven research

Q: My research doesn't involve animals or people. What ethical considerations apply?

Remember: There is no research free from ethical considerations

Consider: Data handling, environmental impact, resource allocation, potential misuse, who benefits/who might be harmed, social justice implications

Q: Can I use humor in my video?

Yes, but use judgment:
  • Appropriate for most topics if tasteful
  • Avoid for serious topics (terminal illness, human suffering)
  • Keep it light and relevant
  • Don't let humor undermine seriousness of your work
