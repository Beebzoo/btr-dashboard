Before You Start the BTR: Complete Checklist

You are about to embark on the final step of the Maastricht Science Programme ‚Äì the Bachelor Thesis Research. This is your opportunity to demonstrate how much you have grown and learned throughout the past two and a half years. The BTR is where you bring together all your knowledge, skills, and scientific curiosity to conduct independent research that contributes to your field. Finding the right project and supervisor can feel daunting at first, but remember: patience and persistence always lead to a great, suitable project! Whether you choose to stay in Maastricht, explore opportunities elsewhere in the Netherlands, or venture abroad, this journey will be one of the most rewarding experiences of your academic career. Follow the steps below carefully to ensure a smooth application process and set yourself up for success.

Am I Eligible to Start the BTR?
‚ñº

Credit Requirements

You need at least 145 ECTS to start your BTR (out of 150 non-BTR credits)

Your situation:

‚úì 150+ ECTS: Free to start BTR. No additional module necessary.
‚úì Exactly 145 ECTS: Free to start BTR. Must apply for an additional module to graduate on time (see Section 6 for details).
‚ö†Ô∏è Below 145 ECTS: Provisionally accepted to start BTR only if courses and/or projects are currently under grading. Contact your Academic Advisor to confirm your eligibility.

GPA Requirements (For BTR Abroad Only)

If you plan to conduct your BTR outside of Maastricht:

  • Required GPA: Nominal 7.5 GPA
  • Below 7.5? Contact the BTR Office (msp-btr@maastrichtuniversity.nl) to discuss your options. Exceptions may be possible depending on circumstances.

Course Completion Requirements (For BTR Abroad)

  • All courses must be completed before going abroad
  • Projects must be completed or under final grading
Not sure about your eligibility?

Schedule a meeting with your Academic Advisor to confirm your credits and discuss your BTR plans.

Finding a Research Supervisor
‚ñº

Finding a thesis topic and Research Supervisor is entirely YOUR responsibility. Be curious, persistent, and don't be afraid to send emails!

Where to Look for Supervisors

Option 1: Canvas Research Opportunities

  • Navigate to Canvas > MSP Students > Research Areas of MSP & UM Staff
  • Browse open BTR projects from MSP faculty
  • Scroll down for research opportunities outside MSP but within UM

Option 2: BTR Advertisement Board

  • Physical board located on the 1st floor, opposite the lifts
  • Features current BTR opportunities from various departments
  • Updated regularly with new projects

Option 3: Your Own Initiative

  • Contact faculty members whose research interests align with yours
  • Reach out to supervisors from previous projects or courses
  • Use the internet and academic papers to identify research groups
  • Send professional emails expressing your interest

Research Supervisor Requirements

  • Generally expected to have a PhD
  • Should have several years of research experience
  • Must be willing to supervise throughout your BTR period
  • For external BTR: Supervisor must be affiliated with a university or research institution
Note: PhD students may act as day-to-day supervisors but should not be the official Research Supervisor with final responsibility.

Finding Supervisors at Different Locations

Within MSP (Maastricht University):

  • Close at hand for regular meetings
  • May allow continuation from previous project work
  • Often already familiar with MSP requirements
  • Check BTR Advertisement Board and Canvas

Within Maastricht (Outside MSP):

  • Other UM faculties (e.g., Biobased, DKE, M4I)
  • Still close for additional modules if needed
  • May offer specialized equipment or expertise
  • Often already supervisors of MSP projects or courses

üåç Anywhere in the World (Abroad):

  • Opportunity to enhance your network
  • Experience science in different environment
  • Requirements: 7.5 GPA, all courses completed
  • Identify groups through internet, papers, conferences
  • Contact potential supervisors directly
üåç Special Requirements for BTR Abroad:
  • Must have completed all courses
  • Nominal 7.5 GPA required
  • Physical presence at host institution required (no remote/online BTR)
  • External agreement must be signed before starting

Tips for Contacting Potential Supervisors

  1. Research their work before reaching out
  2. Write professional, concise emails
  3. Clearly express your interests and how they align with their research
  4. Be prepared to discuss your academic background
  5. Follow up if you don't hear back within 1-2 weeks
  6. Be patient ‚Äì finding the right match takes time!

  2. Research question/objectives: What you aim to investigate
  3. Research techniques/methods: Approaches you will use
  4. Expected outcomes: What your research may contribute

Course Registration & Backup Course (CRITICAL)
‚ñº

Why You Need a Backup Course

Because the BTR approval process takes at least 3 weeks, you must register for a backup course before the Course Registration deadline. This ensures you are registered for courses in case your BTR is not approved in time.

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: This is YOUR Responsibility

What you MUST do:

  1. Register for ONE backup course before the Course Registration deadline
  2. IF your BTR is approved: YOU must withdraw yourself from the backup course
  3. IF you don't withdraw: The course will remain on your schedule and a FAIL will appear on your transcript if you don't attend/complete it

Step-by-Step Process

Before BTR Approval:

  1. Choose an appropriate backup course (typically a Period 1 or Period 4 course)
  2. Register for this course during the Course Registration period
  3. Submit your BTR Request to Start on Collent

After BTR Approval:

  1. Receive approval email from BTR Office
  2. Immediately withdraw yourself from the backup course
  3. Confirm withdrawal is processed in your student portal

If BTR NOT Approved in Time:

  1. Attend the backup course as planned
  2. Reapply for BTR for the next start period

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Assuming the BTR Office will withdraw you from the backup course
‚ùå Forgetting to register for a backup course at all
‚ùå Registering for multiple backup courses (only 1 is needed)
‚ùå Missing the withdrawal deadline after BTR approval
‚ö†Ô∏è REMEMBER: The BTR Office does NOT withdraw you from backup courses. This is entirely YOUR responsibility. Set a reminder for yourself!

Additional Module: Reaching 180 ECTS
‚ñº

Many students need to take an additional module alongside their BTR to reach the required 180 ECTS for graduation.

Who Needs an Additional Module?

If you start your BTR with exactly 145 ECTS, you will need to complete an additional 5 ECTS module during your BTR to graduate on time (145 + 30 BTR + 5 additional = 180 total).

Important Restrictions

Location Requirement:
  • Additional modules are ONLY allowed if your BTR is in or near Maastricht
  • You cannot take an additional module if doing BTR abroad
  • Must be practical to attend classes alongside BTR work

Module Limits:

  • Maximum ONE additional module during entire BTR
  • Maximum 5 ECTS
  • Cannot be taken in first year or first two semesters of studies

Approval Process (Multiple Steps Required)

Step 1: Research Supervisor Approval

‚è∞ BEFORE you start your BTR

  • Your Research Supervisor must approve that you are taking an additional module
  • Discuss how you will manage the workload
  • Supervisor is not obligated to approve

Step 2: Board of Examiners (BoE) Approval

‚è∞ By the Course Registration Deadline

  • Submit Additional Module Request via Intranet > Forms > Additional Module
  • BoE will evaluate based on your GPA and Progress Rate (PR)
  • Your BTR must already be approved before BoE will consider additional module request

Step 3: Course Registration

  • Include the additional module on your Course Registration form
  • Register for both your backup course AND the additional module
  • If BTR approved: withdraw from backup, keep additional module

What If You Fail the Additional Module?

‚ö†Ô∏è If you need a resit for the additional module, this can delay your graduation date.
  • Resits typically occur after the BTR end date
  • You may not graduate in the same ceremony as your cohort
  • Plan accordingly and take the additional module seriously
üìã Additional Module Checklist:
Confirmed with Research Supervisor (BEFORE BTR starts)
Submitted Additional Module request to BoE (by Course Reg deadline)
Noted on RTS form
Registered for backup course + additional module
Plan to withdraw from backup course after BTR approval

Legal Agreements for External/Abroad BTR
‚ñº

If you are conducting your BTR outside of Maastricht University, several legal agreements must be completed and signed BEFORE you can start.

External Agreement (MANDATORY) ‚ö†Ô∏è

Required for: ALL students doing BTR outside of Maastricht University

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: You CANNOT start your BTR until this agreement is fully signed.

What is it?

A legally binding agreement between:

  1. Maastricht University
  2. The host institution (company/university/research center)
  3. You (the student)

Purpose:

  • Clarifies responsibilities of all parties
  • Establishes liability and insurance coverage
  • Confirms supervision arrangements
  • Outlines intellectual property rights

Timeline Requirements

Minimum 3 weeks before BTR start date:
  • Submit all legal documents to BTR Office for review
  • Allow time for legal checking
  • Allow time for negotiations if needed
  • Allow time for signatures from all parties

‚ö†Ô∏è Documents submitted less than 1 month before your start date are considered too close and may cause delays or prevent you from starting on time.

How to Complete the External Agreement

Step 1: Download the Template

Available on Canvas: MSP Students > Bachelor Thesis Research > Forms

Step 2: Fill in Required Information

University Section:

  • Institution: Maastricht University
  • Faculty/Department: Faculty of Science and Engineering / Maastricht Science Programme
  • Address: Paul-Henri Spaaklaan 1, 6229 EN, Maastricht
  • Email: msp-btr@maastrichtuniversity.nl

Step 3: Submit to BTR Office

  • Email to: msp-btr@maastrichtuniversity.nl
  • Subject line: "External Agreement - [Your Name] - [Start Date]"
  • Attach completed form
  • Submit at least 3 weeks before start date

Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs)

Increasingly common, especially with industry partners and private companies.

What to do:

  1. Host institution provides their NDA template
  2. Submit to BTR Office for legal checking
  3. BTR Office coordinates with UM legal department
  4. Cannot be signed without UM approval
  5. Allow extra time for NDA review (3+ weeks)

What Happens If Agreements Aren't Signed in Time?

  • Your BTR start date will be delayed
  • You may need to postpone to the next cohort (6 months later)
  • You will need to maintain course registration
üìã Legal Documents Checklist:
Downloaded External Agreement template
Completed all sections of External Agreement
Submitted to BTR Office (3+ weeks before start)
Obtained NDA from host (if required)
Submitted NDA for legal review
Collected all necessary signatures
Kept copies of all signed documents

Questions about legal agreements? Contact: msp-btr@maastrichtuniversity.nl

Going Abroad: What to Do After BTR Approval
‚ñº

Congratulations on your BTR approval! If you're heading abroad, there are several important steps to complete before departure.

Contact the International Relations Office (IRO)

‚ö†Ô∏è MANDATORY: Contact IRO AFTER receiving BTR approval

Email: msp-iro@maastrichtuniversity.nl

When to contact: Immediately after receiving your BTR approval email from the BTR Office

Emergency Contact Form (MANDATORY)

Required for: ALL students leaving the Netherlands for BTR

What is it?

  • Form providing emergency contact information
  • Allows UM to reach you in case of emergency
  • Allows UM to notify your emergency contacts if needed

Erasmus+ Grant Application (EU Destinations Only)

Eligibility:

  • BTR at a partner institution in the EU or UK
  • Must be Erasmus+ partner university

What it covers:

  • Partial coverage of living expenses abroad
  • Monthly grant amount varies by destination country
  • Does not cover all costs ‚Äì prepare additional budget

Practical Preparations

Passport & Visa:

  • Ensure passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond your return date
  • For non-EU destinations: Obtain necessary visa well in advance (can take 2-3 months)
  • For EU destinations: Check if residence permit needs renewal

Health Insurance:

  • Confirm your health insurance covers you abroad
  • Obtain European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) for EU destinations
  • Consider additional travel insurance
  • Some host institutions require specific insurance coverage

Finances:

Prepare detailed budget including:

  • Rent and utilities
  • Food and daily expenses
  • Travel costs (flights, local transport)
  • Research/lab fees (if applicable)
  • Emergency funds

Contacted IRO immediately after BTR approval
Completed Emergency Contact Form
Applied for Erasmus+ grant (if eligible)
Checked passport validity (6+ months)
Applied for visa (if needed)
Arranged health insurance coverage
Secured accommodation
Created detailed budget

Questions? Contact IRO: msp-iro@maastrichtuniversity.nl

You've submitted your Request to Start ‚Äì great work! Here's what happens next and how to prepare for your BTR journey.

Approval Process

Timeline:

  • Minimum 3 weeks after RTS submission
  • May be longer during busy periods (July-August, December-January)

Internal Advisor Assignment

When: Before you submit your proposal (approximately 2 weeks before BTR start)

Role of Internal Advisor:

  • Grades your proposal alongside Research Supervisor
  • Grades your final thesis alongside Research Supervisor
  • Grades your BTR video (50%) alongside peer review (50%)
  • Meets with you for progress check-in after proposal submission

Information Booklet

When: Approximately 2 weeks before your BTR start date

What's included:

  • Detailed BTR timeline and deadlines
  • Grading rubrics and assessment criteria
  • Submission instructions for Collent
  • Contact information for BTR Office and Internal Advisor
  • FAQs and troubleshooting

BTR Timeline After Approval

Typical September Start (dates are approximate):

  • September 1: BTR Start Date
