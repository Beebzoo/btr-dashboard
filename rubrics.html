Research Proposal Rubric

Assessed by Research Supervisor and Internal Advisor

Assessment Overview

Total Points: 30 | Grade Weight: 15% | Max Word Count: 2,000 words

1. Project Background and Literature Review (10 points)

Exceeds Expectations (5): Project background is explained and put in context of the literature proficiently. Understanding is advanced with higher-level connections. Literature is extensive and curated. Work is at Master student level. {10/10}
Above Average (4): Project background is well explained and contextualized. Minimal misunderstanding, easily fixable. Literature support is good with room for small improvements. Very good quality work above bachelor standards. {8/10}
Sufficient (3): Project background explained to some degree with context. Some scientific misunderstanding and minor mistakes. Literature adequate but has room for improvement. Sufficient for bachelor expectations. {6/10}

2. Research Question and Aims (3 points)

Questions and aims should be fully outlined with minimal imprecision. Clear profiling that exceeds expectations for bachelor level demonstrates thorough planning.

3. Methodological Approach (3 points)

Methods should be fully outlined with limitations accounted for. Approach must show neat understanding from the student.

4. Time Planning (2 points)

Timeline must be clear, understandable, realistic within BTR framework, and account for potential setbacks.

5. Outcome, Significance and Impact (3 points)

Discussion should be compelling with clear narrative. Above expectations means comprehensive coverage without need for improvement.

6. Ethical Issues (2 points)

All relevant ethical issues must be fully discussed at appropriate bachelor level depth.

7. References (2 points)

References must be relevant, from reputable sources, properly formatted, supporting all statements that require literature backing.

8. Overall Details (3 points)

Proposal should have excellent flow with great narrative care, well-structured from start to finish.

9. Layout (2 points)

Layout (sections, titles, formatting) should be designed and curated with content, appearing professional.

10. Spelling/Grammar (2 points)

Proposal must flow smoothly with no significant mistakes, clearly checked and revised before submission.

Midterm Practical Work Rubric

Assessed by Research Supervisor only

Assessment Overview

Total Points: 32 | Grade Weight: 15% | Assessment: Mid-November

1. Practical Skills (5 points)

Exceeds Expectations (5): Exceptional practical skills with consistent and proficient application of techniques and methodologies. Work demonstrates creativity, precision, and thoroughness. At Master student level. {10/10}
Above Average (4): Above-average practical skills with proper application of techniques. General grasp on experimental design. Generally competent despite room for improvement. {8/10}
Sufficient (3): Adequate practical skills with some guided attempt on experimental design. Some inconsistencies or minor errors. Sufficient for bachelor expectations. {6/10}

2. Independence (5 points)

Student should be capable of working autonomously with reasonable efficiency, requiring only occasional feedback and clarification.

3. Initiative (5 points)

Student fulfills basic requirements and is willing to be guided with suggestions. Shows motivation to explore beyond minimum when prompted.

4. Time Management (3 points)

Generally plans and allocates time adequately. Not always independent on deadlines but satisfactory in end goals.

5. Problem Solving (3 points)

Shows basic understanding of problems and applies appropriate problem-solving strategies with some prompting for alternatives.

6. Awareness of Aims (3 points)

Satisfactory understanding of research objectives with basic connection between activities and broader research goals.

7. Recording (e.g., Lab Notebook) (2 points)

Recording should be complete, accurate, and well-organized, documenting all relevant information effectively.

8. Carefulness with Resources (2 points)

Demonstrates careful and responsible use of research materials, equipment, and data.

9. Communication (2 points)

Maintains regular and clear communication channels with supervisor, addressing questions or concerns effectively.

10. Teamwork Skills (2 points)

Actively collaborates with supervisor, lab mates, and technicians, contributing to team goals and sharing responsibilities.

Final Practical Work Rubric

Assessed by Research Supervisor only

Assessment Overview

Total Points: 32 | Grade Weight: 35% | Includes: Defence Presentation

1. Practical Skills (5 points)

Techniques and methodologies should be applied consistently and proficiently with clear understanding of experimental design.

2. Ability to Work Independently (5 points)

Student should demonstrate satisfactory capability for independent working, needing feedback and clarification but making steady progress.

3. Precision and Quality of Work (3 points)

Research conducted with acceptable attention to detail, resulting in reasonably reliable findings. Methods appropriate with minor mistakes.

4. Time Management and Planning (3 points)

Generally plans and allocates time adequately, ensuring completion of tasks within acceptable timeframes.

5. Problem Solving (3 points)

Shows basic understanding of problems and applies appropriate strategies, approaching alternatives with prompting.

6. Safety and Work Ethic (2 points)

Demonstrates strong commitment to safety and ethics, implementing appropriate measures and adhering to guidelines.

7. Detailed Recording of Work and Results (2 points)

Recording should be complete, accurate, and well-organized, effectively documenting all relevant information.

8. Teamwork Skills and Communication (2 points)

Actively collaborates and maintains clear communication channels with all stakeholders.

9. Project Closure and Data Transfer (2 points)

Project wrapped up properly: laboratory/practical environment clean, computer sets organized, data tidy and transferred.

10. Overall Quality of Defence Presentation (5 points)

Exceeds Expectations (5): Exceptional quality with clear and engaging delivery, effective visual aids, excellent organization, and comprehensive understanding. At Master student level. {10/10}
Above Average (4): Well-structured content, effective communication, appropriate visual aids, and solid understanding. Above bachelor standards. {8/10}

Final Thesis Rubric

Assessed by Research Supervisor and Internal Advisor

Assessment Overview

Total Points: 26 | Grade Weight: 25% | Max File Size: 25 MB

1. Overall Scientific Content (5 points)

Exceeds Expectations (5): Groundwork proficiently outlined, narrative and scientific content complete and thorough. Comparable to master/postgraduate level. {10/10}
Above Average (4): Work clear with good literature support and narrative demonstrating understanding. Minor points for improvement. Very good quality above bachelor average. {8/10}

2. Integration of Research Proposal and Aims (3 points)

Proposed work should be clear and seamlessly integrated, connecting introduction to results with impactful stated aims.

3. Analysis of the Results (3 points)

Results arranged with purpose, clear flow and consistent approach. Statistical analysis, tabulation, and other processes performed properly.

4. Discussion and Interpretation of Results (2 points)

All results, their significance and impact proficiently discussed and contextualized within the bigger picture.

5. Scientific Argumentation (3 points)

Every point approached in rigorous scientific manner with appropriate tone and clear flow, demonstrating proficient understanding.

6. Presentation of Graphical Material (2 points)

Graphics, schematics, tables, and images presented with consistent effort, adding to thesis and highlighting critical thinking.

7. General Layout and Presentation (2 points)

Layout (sections, titles, formatting) designed and curated with content, appearing professional.

8. Spelling and Grammar (2 points)

Thesis flows smoothly with no mistakes, clearly checked, revised and edited before submission.

9. Personal Critical Reflection (2 points)

Reflection addresses overall 4-month process, taking into account all aspects in personal manner with constructive view of future.

10. References (2 points)

Relevant references from reputable sources, properly formatted, supporting all statements requiring literature backing.

BTR Video Assessment Rubric

Assessed by Internal Advisor and Second Assessor

Assessment Overview

Total Points: 26 | Grade Weight: 10% | Length: 5-7 minutes

1. Clear Opening and Concluding Statements (2 points)

Video must include well-structured opening with student introduction, engaging topic introduction, and concise summary/call-to-action conclusion.

2. Work and Its Background Clearly Described (3 points)

Exceeds Expectations (3): Exceptional and engaging description introducing topic and field to general audience with no prior context. Includes state-of-the-art considerations with clear narrative highlighting relevance and impact. {10/10}
Above Average (2.5): Solid description effectively introducing topic and field to general audience. Includes relevant state-of-the-art considerations. Good understanding and communication. {8/10}

3. Scientific Importance Well Discussed (3 points)

Should provide thorough discussion of scientific importance, explaining relevance, novelty, and potential impact accessible to general audience.

4. Societal Importance Well Discussed (3 points)

Should comprehensively discuss how research addresses real-world challenges, benefits society, or contributes to broader societal goals.

5. Discussion of Ethical Issues (2 points)

Must fully and coherently discuss ethical issues, addressing considerations, consequences, and ethical frameworks.

6. Video Suitable for Non-Scientists (3 points)

Exceptionally well-suited for non-scientists, presenting complex concepts clearly. Avoids jargon, uses relatable examples and visual aids.

7. Correct Amount of Details (2 points)

Coherent and well-paced, balancing details to be neither overwhelming nor oversimplified, ensuring clarity for general audience.

8. Presentation of the Video (3 points)

Student delivers clear and engaging performance with confident, articulate speaking style and appropriate body language. Professional production quality.

9. Style Appropriate to Message (2 points)

Video style well-aligned with message, effectively conveying content through appropriate visual and narrative elements.

10. Graphics of the Video (3 points)

Exceptional graphic design with visually captivating presentation. Visuals (animations, images, text) enhance understanding and engagement.
