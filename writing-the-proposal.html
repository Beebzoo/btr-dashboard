Overview & Purpose

What is the BTR Proposal?

The BTR proposal is a research plan that outlines what you intend to investigate during your Bachelor Thesis Research. It demonstrates your ability to:

  • Identify a relevant research question
  • Review existing literature in your field
  • Design appropriate research methods
  • Think critically about your research approach
  • Communicate your ideas clearly in academic writing
Important Note: Your proposal is a plan, not a promise. It's normal for your research to evolve as you progress. The proposal demonstrates your preparation and thinking at the start of your BTR.

Why is it Important?

The proposal serves multiple purposes:

  • Planning Tool: Helps you organize your thoughts and approach before diving into research
  • Communication: Ensures you and your supervisor are aligned on the research direction
  • Assessment: Worth 15% of your final BTR grade
  • Reference Document: Guides your work throughout the BTR period

Proposal Structure

Your proposal should include the following sections. The exact structure may vary slightly depending on your field, so discuss with your supervisor.

1. Title Page

Include:

  • Proposed thesis title
  • Your name and student number
  • Research Supervisor name and affiliation
  • Internal Advisor name (if known at submission)
  • Date of submission
  • Word count (excluding references)

2. Introduction & Background

Length: ~400-500 words

Content:

  • Introduce your research topic and its relevance
  • Provide context: Why is this topic important?
  • What is the current state of knowledge in this area?
  • What gap in knowledge does your research address?
  • Clearly state your research question(s) and/or hypothesis
Writing Tip: Start broad (general context) and narrow down to your specific research question. Think of it as a funnel: background ‚Üí gap ‚Üí your research.

3. Literature Review

Length: ~600-700 words

Content:

  • Review relevant scientific literature in your research area
  • Demonstrate your understanding of the field
  • Identify what is known and what remains unclear
  • Show how previous studies relate to your research question
  • Explain theoretical frameworks or concepts you'll use

Tips:

  • Use recent peer-reviewed sources (ideally within last 5-10 years)
  • Synthesize information rather than just listing studies
  • Be critical: discuss strengths and limitations of previous work
  • Minimum 10-15 quality references expected

4. Research Methods

Length: ~600-700 words

Content:

  • Research Design: What type of research will you conduct? (experimental, observational, computational, etc.)
  • Materials: What equipment, samples, or data will you use?
  • Procedures: Step-by-step description of your research approach
  • Analysis: How will you analyze your results? (statistical methods, software, techniques)
  • Timeline: Brief overview of your research schedule

Be specific:

  • Include actual techniques, protocols, or models you'll use
  • Mention specific software or equipment
  • Explain why these methods are appropriate for your research question
Common Mistake: Being too vague in methods. Don't just say "I will analyze the data." Specify HOW you'll analyze it and WITH WHAT tools.

5. Expected Outcomes & Significance

Length: ~200-300 words

Content:

  • What results do you anticipate? (Be realistic and cautious)
  • How might your findings contribute to the field?
  • What are the potential applications or implications?
  • Acknowledge limitations or challenges you may face

6. References

Not counted in word limit

Content:

  • Complete list of all sources cited in APA format (or approved alternative)
  • Use a reference manager (Mendeley, Zotero, EndNote) to ensure consistency
  • Double-check formatting before submission

Grading & Assessment

Who Grades Your Proposal?

Your proposal is graded by:

  • Research Supervisor (50%)
  • Internal Advisor (50%)

The final grade is the average of both assessors' grades.

Proposal Weight in Final BTR Grade

The proposal accounts for 15% of your total BTR grade (30 ECTS).

Assessment Criteria

Your proposal will be evaluated on:

1. Research Question & Objectives (20%)

  • Is the research question clear and focused?
  • Is it scientifically relevant and feasible?
  • Are objectives specific and achievable?

2. Literature Review (25%)

  • Demonstrates understanding of relevant literature
  • Critical analysis of previous work
  • Identifies knowledge gap effectively
  • Uses appropriate and recent sources

3. Research Methods (30%)

  • Methods are appropriate for the research question
  • Sufficient detail and justification provided
  • Shows understanding of techniques and analysis
  • Realistic timeline and feasibility

4. Academic Writing & Structure (25%)

  • Clear, concise, and logical organization
  • Proper use of academic language
  • Correct grammar, spelling, and punctuation
  • Proper citations and reference formatting
  • Adherence to word limit and format requirements
Feedback: You'll receive detailed feedback from both assessors along with your grade. Use this feedback to improve your final thesis!

What if My Proposal Grade is Low?

If you receive a failing grade (below 5.5) or are unsatisfied with your proposal grade:

  • Review the feedback carefully
  • Discuss concerns with your Research Supervisor
  • Contact your Internal Advisor for clarification
  • Use the feedback to improve your final thesis

Important: There is no resit for the proposal. The grade stands, but strong performance in other BTR components can compensate.
